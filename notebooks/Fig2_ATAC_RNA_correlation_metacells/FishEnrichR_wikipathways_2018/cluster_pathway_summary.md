## Summary

This FishEnrichR pathway analysis reveals distinct functional clusters during zebrafish embryogenesis:

**Early Patterning (Clusters 0, 2, 3):** BMP/Wnt/Nodal signaling pathways establish embryonic axes and specify neural crest fate

**Tissue Differentiation (Clusters 4, 5):** Mesenchymal and muscle development programs drive skeletal and contractile tissue formation

**Physiological Regulation (Clusters 6, 8):** Calcium homeostasis and Wnt modulation maintain cellular function and signaling balance

**Hematopoiesis (Cluster 7):** Blood cell development and cytokine responses establish the hematopoietic system

**Neural Signaling (Clusters 1, 9):** Neurotransmitter synthesis and GPCR signaling enable neural communication

**Growth Responses (Cluster 10):** FGF/MAPK signaling cascades coordinate cellular responses to growth factors

**Cell Proliferation (Cluster 11):** Cell cycle and protein synthesis machinery support rapid embryonic growth

**Metabolism (Clusters 12, 13):** Lipid metabolism and metabolic regulation maintain cellular energy homeostasis

The pathway enrichments clearly map to key developmental processes occurring during early zebrafish embryogenesis, from initial axis formation through tissue-specific differentiation programs.
